Autoregressively Decoding Guitar Effects Chain

Goals

The gols for this independent study was twofold. First, I wanted to familiarize myself with machine learning concepts and PyTorch through a hands on project. With the recent developments with transformer based LLMs, I wanted a deeper dive into the transformer architecture. Second, I wanted to apply the techniques in a Music Information Retrieval (MIR) task. As a novice electric guitarist, one of learning hurdles that I've yet to conquer is shaping the tone of the guitar with the use of an effects chain. Upon taking Natural Language Processing, I wanted to explore whether a statistical model could tackle the problem of audio effect parameter estimation.

Background

In music production, special audio effects can be applied to dry audio to modify timbre, pitch, time, and etc. to enhance musical expression or musicality of the audio. For electric guitars, this can be done with a pedal board, which is collection of pedals chained together into an effects chain. A pedal is a circuit designed to alter the incoming audio to create different effects, ranging from distortion, chorus, compression, reverb, and so on. Each pedal has set of parameters, usually in forms of physical knobs, that controls how and/or how much the pedal influence incoming signal. Figure 1 illustrates a simpiflied model of this system.
